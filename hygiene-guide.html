Menstrual Hygiene

What is Menstrual Hygiene?

Menstrual hygiene refers to practices and products used to manage menstruation effectively and maintain personal cleanliness during this period. Proper hygiene helps prevent infections and discomfort.

Menstrual Products

Click to Show Menstrual Products

1. **Pads**: Absorbent materials worn in underwear to collect menstrual blood.

2. **Tampons**: Absorbent products inserted into the vagina to absorb menstrual blood.

3. **Menstrual Cups**: Flexible cups inserted into the vagina to collect menstrual blood.

4. **Period Underwear**: Specialized underwear designed to absorb menstrual blood.

Hygiene Tips

Click to Show Hygiene Tips

1. **Change Products Regularly**: Change pads, tampons, or menstrual cups every 4-6 hours to maintain hygiene and prevent leaks.

2. **Wash Hands Frequently**: Wash your hands before and after changing menstrual products to reduce the risk of infections.

3. **Clean Genital Area**: Wash the genital area with mild soap and water daily. Avoid douching as it can disrupt the natural balance.

4. **Wear Breathable Fabrics**: Opt for cotton underwear and avoid tight-fitting clothing to reduce irritation.

5. **Store Products Properly**: Keep menstrual products in a clean, dry place to prevent contamination.

When to See a Doctor

If you experience severe discomfort, unusual symptoms, or persistent infections, consult a healthcare professional for advice and treatment.
