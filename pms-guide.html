Understanding PMS

What is PMS?

Premenstrual Syndrome (PMS) refers to a range of physical and emotional symptoms that occur in the lead-up to menstruation. Symptoms typically start a few days before your period and end within a few days of its onset.

Common Symptoms

1. Emotional symptoms: mood swings, irritability, and depression.

2. Physical symptoms: bloating, breast tenderness, and headaches.

3. Behavioral symptoms: changes in sleep patterns and appetite.

Possible Causes

The exact cause of PMS is not known, but it may be related to hormonal changes during the menstrual cycle. Factors like stress, genetics, and lifestyle also play a role.

Management and Remedies

1. **Diet**: Eating a balanced diet with plenty of fruits, vegetables, and whole grains can help manage symptoms.

2. **Exercise**: Regular physical activity can reduce the severity of PMS symptoms.

3. **Stress Management**: Techniques such as yoga, meditation, and deep breathing exercises can be beneficial.

4. **Medication**: Over-the-counter pain relievers and hormonal treatments can alleviate symptoms for some women.
